Welcome. This is a book about Loop Engineering, and over the next several chapters we're going to explore it together from the ground up.

So what is Loop Engineering? At its heart, it's the practice of designing systems that prompt, observe, verify, and iterate AI coding agents on their own, automatically, rather than guiding them by hand, one turn at a time. Instead of sitting there typing instruction after instruction, you build a small system that drives the agent for you. That's the idea we'll keep coming back to.

The chapters ahead are arranged in a deliberate learning arc. We start with the foundations and the concepts, so that by the time you reach the hands-on, build-oriented material, you already understand what a Loop is and why it works the way it does. You can listen straight through for a complete course, or move to whatever topic matters most to you in the moment.

We'll begin with an introduction that lays out what this book teaches and the goals you'll achieve along the way. From there we'll talk about who this book is really for, and the background knowledge it assumes you bring with you. Then we'll define the discipline itself, looking closely at how Loop Engineering contrasts with the manual, turn-by-turn prompting most people are used to.

After that, we'll trace where all of this came from. We'll follow the evolution from prompt engineering into loop engineering, and unpack its relationship to a pattern known as ReAct. We'll also look at the origins of the term, and the earlier technique that helped inspire it, the Ralph Loop.

With the groundwork in place, we'll move into anatomy and flow. We'll take a deep dive into each of the eight building blocks that make up a Loop, and then walk through the loop lifecycle, the end-to-end flow, and that crucial decision point where a Loop chooses whether to continue, to spawn, or to stop.

Then comes the practical heart of the book. We'll build a Loop together, working through both a minimal example and a production-grade one, and exploring patterns for setting clear goals and sensible guardrails.

Finally, we'll turn to judgment and synthesis. We'll talk about the real benefits of Loop Engineering and the kinds of work it's genuinely suited for. We'll be honest about the risks and limitations too, including cost, the problem of low-quality output, and the judgment that Loops still demand from us. And we'll close by drawing it all together, framing Loop Engineering as what it truly is: a small, well-designed control system.

Along the way you'll find supporting material as well, including a glossary that serves as the single source of truth for the book's terminology, and a collection of references and claims drawn from the source material, each one flagged so you can verify it for yourself.
